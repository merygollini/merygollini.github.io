    An Exhibit

Introdution

This Webpage was realized as a final project for a Laboratory class at the University of Bologna. The aim of this project is the creation of a virtual environment to dispaly a photographic exhibit. In addition, IIIF Thecnology has been used to better display the images and provide a more complete way to observe them.

The pictures that I have chosen represent some of the most important moments in the the history of photography. They have been taken with different cameras, in different times and show different scenarios by pioneers of photography

3D Modeling

I have created two different 3D models. The first is a room hosting a photo gallery, made with Blender. The secoond one is an object reconstruction made with Zephyr Aerial.

The 3D models have been uploaded on P3D.IN

IIIF Technology

The IIF is an interoperable protocol to share images.

Due to the quality of some of the photographs some images are hard to see and to study.

The III technology in this case can greatly support the analysis of those images. In fact, once an image has been associated with an IIIF URI API, it is possible to access it directly online and to manipulate it directly. This is the URI template: {scheme}://{server}{/prefix}/{identifier}/{region}/{size}/{rotation}/{quality}.{format}

To achieve this the images have been uploaded to IIIF Hosting, a platform that can host images and generate a URI compliant with the IIIF Schema.

First photo ever taken.

This is the first photo we conserve and it was taken by Joseph Nicéphore Niépce in 1826 with a technique known as heliography.

First photo with people

First photo with people we conserve. It was taken in 1839 by Louis Daguerre.

First self-portrait

Taken in Philadelphia bt Robert Cornelius in 1839. He sat for a little over one minute before covering the lens.

First photo of Rome

First photo of the city of Rome.It was taken in 1842.

Only photo of Porto di Ripetta

Porto di Ripetta, Rome, by Fratelli Alessandri, circa 1850. There isa no record of this port aside from this photo. It was demolished in the 1880s.

First photo of trulli

First photo of Trulli. It was taken in 1900. It is also one of the first photographs ever taken in Italy.

First color photograph

First color photograph. It was taken in 1855, by James Clerk Maxwell, who developped the three-color method.

First underwater photo with colors

First underwater photograph in color. It was taken in 1926 National Geographic photographer Charles Martin and botanist William Longley by encasing the camera in a waterproof housing.

First digital photo

First digital photo. It was taken in 1957 by Russell Kirsch by making a digital scan of a film image.
